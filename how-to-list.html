How do I post my listing?

For both property owner (FSBO) and Agent, posting your listing is easy.

Step 1:Login/Sign Up

Create a FREE account by clicking the Login/Sign up button located at the top righthand corner of the page. If you already have an account, LOGIN with your username and password.

OR

To create an account, click SIGN UP. (Note: You must SIGN UP as an FSBO or agent to advertise a property).

Step 2: Dashboard

You will then be automatically redirected to your personal and private DASHBOARD page where you will be able to purchase the advertising space, save properties, view ad performance and much more.

Step 3: Purchase property ad space

Click the button at the top left side of your dashboard “click here to purchase new property ad space”.

Choose your package, select your feature upgrades, confirm your order and checkout.

Your NEW ad space will now show up under your My Properties Tab as “Awaiting Action”.

Step 4: My Listings

Select your property “Awaiting Action”. Click Edit Property. Then select and fill out all applicable property information, upload photos, documents and videos. Click Submit.

To display properly – all photos should have a minimum width of 770px and a minimum height of 386px.

Do I rename my photos? YES! To have your photos show up in local searches, be sure to use your search terms and location in the file name. ie. ‘Horse farm for sale Ontario.jpg’ or ‘horse acreage in Alberta.jpg’

I have sold or rented my property, now what?

Your ad space is yours for the full package duration. If you sell your property you can mark it SOLD and use it for advertising or swap that property with a new one at NO extra charge! If you wish to remove your current ad and not replace it, that is ok too!

What qualifies as a Horse Property?

Canadian Horse Properties.com qualifies properties of 3.0 Acres or more as Horse Properties. Typical Canadian rural bylaws require a minimum of 3.0 acres to accommodate a horse.

Please be sure to check with your local municipality to ensure your property qualifies as a horse property.
